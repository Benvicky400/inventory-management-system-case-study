 Inventory Management System – Business Analysis Case Study
 **Introduction**

This project is a Business Analysis case study focused on designing an Inventory Management System that improves how retail businesses track, manage, and control stock.

It was created as part of my portfolio to demonstrate practical skills in business analysis, including requirements gathering, process modeling, system design, and documentation.

The system simulates a real-world retail environment where inventory accuracy, purchase order management, and stock monitoring are critical for daily operations.

 Problem Statement

Many retail businesses face challenges such as:

Inaccurate inventory tracking
Stock shortages or overstock situations
Inefficient manual processes
Delayed purchase approvals
Poor visibility into stock levels

This system is designed to solve these issues by introducing a structured and automated inventory workflow.

 **Project Objectives**

The goal of this system is to:

Maintain accurate and real-time inventory records
Automate stock level monitoring and alerts
Improve purchase order creation and approval flow
Enhance warehouse and supplier coordination
Support better business decision-making through reporting
 **My Approach**

To design this system, I followed a structured Business Analysis approach:

Identified business needs and pain points
Defined system scope and stakeholders
Mapped end-to-end business processes
Translated requirements into system functions
Designed supporting diagrams and documentation
